Company Background

Founded in 2024, NISBA emerged from recognizing that while Islamic finance is a £2.4 trillion industry, millions of Muslims remain excluded due to complexity and lack of accessible education.

Our mission is to bridge this gap by combining expert education, practical tools, and one-on-one coaching to help Muslims build wealth while staying true to their values.

NISBA's integrated platform consists of four core modules designed to provide comprehensive Islamic finance education and coaching:

Individual Learning Portal

Investment simulator with £50K virtual portfolio, 400+ hours of educational content, progress tracking system, and automated Zakat calculator with guidance

Halal Investment Screener

AI-powered Shariah compliance analysis, real-time stock data, custom filtering options, and regular scholar validation of screening criteria

Personal Coaching System

1-on-1 video coaching sessions, progress monitoring tools, resource sharing capabilities, and detailed performance analytics

Corporate Wellness Portal

Employee dashboards, HR analytics and reporting, white-label branding options, and integration APIs for existing HR systems

Risk Analysis

Comprehensive risk assessment with mitigation strategies and contingency planning

Business Risks

Market Risks

Competition: Large financial institutions entering the Islamic finance education space

Mitigation: Build strong technological and educational moats through scholar partnerships and proprietary algorithms

Economic Downturn: Reduced corporate and individual spending on financial education

Mitigation: Focus on ROI-driven corporate clients and offer flexible pricing models

Technical Risks

Platform Scalability: System failures during rapid user growth periods

Mitigation: Cloud-native architecture with auto-scaling capabilities and comprehensive monitoring

Data Security: Potential breaches affecting user trust and regulatory compliance

Mitigation: SOC 2 compliance, regular security audits, and cyber insurance coverage

Regulatory Risks

Financial Services Regulation: Potential new licensing requirements for financial education

Mitigation: Continuous legal counsel monitoring and focus on education rather than investment advice

Data Protection: GDPR and UK data protection compliance challenges

Mitigation: Privacy-by-design architecture and regular compliance reviews

Operational Risks

Key Person Dependency: Over-reliance on founders and key Islamic scholars

Mitigation: Document all processes and build a strong advisory board with multiple scholar relationships

Coach Quality: Inconsistent coaching delivery affecting user satisfaction

Mitigation: Rigorous training programs, quality monitoring, and performance management systems

Risk Mitigation Strategies

Diversified Revenue Streams

Multiple revenue sources (B2C, B2B, partnerships) reduce dependency on any single income stream

Strong Advisory Board

Access to expertise and networks through experienced advisors in Islamic finance, technology, and business

Robust Technology Infrastructure

Scalable, secure, and reliable platform architecture built for enterprise-grade performance

Conservative Financial Management

Careful cash management, regular financial reviews, and scenario planning for different growth trajectories

Comprehensive Insurance Coverage

Professional indemnity, cyber liability, and directors & officers insurance protection
